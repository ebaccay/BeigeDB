About the Project

The Why

BeigeDB is a public queryable database containing the contents of the Federal Reserve Beige Books. The Beige Books themselves are reports published by twelve separate districts in the Federal Reserve System. These reports contain a summary of information regarding monetary policy, implementations of such monetary policy, and economic conditions regarding different sectors (agriculture, manufacturing, etc.) within the district.

Each entry on BeigeDB also contains special keywords that identify the main topic of the summary than can also be used in your query. All queries processed from the compiled database can also be downloaded in a CSV format for ease to people who are using this data.

This database was created by a research team of three students at UC Berkeley within GoodlyLabs in partnership with the Berkeley Institute for Data Science (BIDS) in the hopes of making hard-to-access/hard-to-parse federal data easily accessible to the public for use in research and similar projects.

Direction and guidance was provided by members of the GoodlyLabs team such as Nick Adams, Johannes Fritz, Aakriti Kaul, and Eve Mwangi. Thank you for supporting and empowering us to complete this project!
